The small intestine is the longest part of the digestive system (~6 meters), extending from the pylorus to the cecum. Its main functions are to complete digestion and absorb nutrients.

A. Duodenum (Ø§Ù„Ø§Ø«Ù†Ø§ Ø¹Ø´Ø±)

The shortest (10 inches), widest part, C-shaped around the pancreatic head. It begins at the pyloroduodenal junction and ends at the duodenojejunal flexure.

1. First Part (Superior)

  • Size: 2 inches (5 cm). First 2 cm are mobile (duodenal cap).
  • Course: Upward and backward at L1.
  • Relations:
    • Anterior: Quadrate lobe of liver, gallbladder.
    • Posterior: Lesser sac, Gastro-duodenal artery, bile duct, portal vein, IVC.
    • Superior: Epiploic foramen, lesser omentum.
    • Inferior: Head of pancreas.

2. Second Part (Descending)

  • Size: 3 inches (7.5 cm).
  • Course: Descends vertically (L2-L3).
  • Key Features:
    • Receives Bile Duct & Main Pancreatic Duct at the Major Duodenal Papilla.
    • Accessory Pancreatic Duct (if present) opens at the Minor Duodenal Papilla.
  • Relations:
    • Anterior: Gallbladder, R. lobe of liver, transverse colon.
    • Posterior: R. kidney hilum, R. ureter, R. renal vessels, IVC, R. psoas.
    • Medial: Head of pancreas, bile duct.

3. Third Part (Horizontal)

  • Size: 4 inches (10 cm).
  • Course: Horizontally to the left at L3.
  • Relations:
    • Anterior: Root of mesentery, Superior Mesenteric Vessels (SMA & SMV).
    • Posterior: R. psoas, IVC, aorta.

4. Fourth Part (Ascending)

  • Size: 1 inch (2.5 cm).
  • Course: Upward and left to the Duodenojejunal Flexure.
  • Key Feature: Held by the Ligament of Treitz.

  • Course: Splenic Flexure to iliac crest.
  • Peritoneum: Retroperitoneal (fixed).
  • Posterior Relations: L. Kidney, L. psoas, iliacus.

  • Nerve Supply: IMA Plexus (Pelvic Splanchnic Nerves).

  • Peritoneum: Intraperitoneal (mobile). Has **Sigmoid Mesocolon**.
  • Anterior Relations (Male): Urinary bladder.
  • Anterior Relations (Female): Uterus, upper vagina.
  • Blood Supply: Sigmoid a. (from IMA).
  • Nerve Supply: Inferior Hypogastric Plexuses.
